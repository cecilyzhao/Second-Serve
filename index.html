Why Food Waste Matters

Every day, restaurants and grocery stores throw away large amounts of edible food. At the same time, students, families, and shelters may struggle to access affordable meals.

The problem is not only food waste. It is also timing, access, and connection. Second Serve is designed to close that gap through a simple real-time food sharing system.

About Our Project

Second Serve is a hyper-local food waste redistribution network. Our goal is to connect restaurants and grocery stores that have surplus food with students, families, shelters, and nearby community members.

Businesses can upload surplus food with a photo, quantity, and pickup time window. Users can then open the website, see nearby food, reserve an item, and pick it up within the available time.

By making food donation faster and easier, Second Serve helps reduce landfill waste, lower methane emissions from food decomposition, and give edible food a second chance to help people.
